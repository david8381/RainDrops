# Purpose

## Project Goal
Build a fast, friendly math game where falling drops are cleared by typing answers. The game should feel responsive, playable immediately, and let the user control their own practice mix while the game is running.

## Design Principles
- Low setup friction: first visit shows a Play/Tutorial/Test Me/player menu, then returning visits load directly into play.
- Fast input: ordinary numeric answers clear immediately; multi-step or symbolic answers use Enter only where needed.
- User-controlled pacing: operation types, per-operation difficulty, fall speed, and active-drop load are adjustable during play.
- Content level and practice pressure are separate mastery axes: levels measure problem scope, while Blitz survival time, Wave max simultaneous load, and Worksheet time give optional fluency goals.
- Clarity over complexity: production remains static HTML/CSS/browser JavaScript with no build step.
- Testable core behavior: math/problem rules live in DOM-free JavaScript so they can be covered by deterministic unit tests.

## Current Player-Facing Behavior
- Drops fall from the sky and clear when the correct answer is typed.
- The player ship is a canvas-drawn vector ship that briefly turns toward solved problems and fires from its nose.
- First-time visitors see a polished welcome menu with Play, Tutorial, Test Me, the current player, and a button into the Login player manager. Choosing Play dismisses the menu for future visits; accepting a Test Me placement also marks the welcome flow seen; the Menu link can reopen it later.
- Test Me is an optional diagnostic placement flow. It starts at level 1 for a selected operation, runs like the regular falling-drop game one problem at a time, repeats missed facts twice, and uses a per-level shield to decide when to move on: correct answers fill the shield and climb to the next level, misses drain it, and an emptied shield recommends the level you stalled on. Harder problem types (Shapes, SI, factoring) fall more slowly so they stay readable. It can set that operation's starting level. Actual Test Me answers are recorded as ordinary practice; lower levels below the accepted start are marked as placement-advanced, with eligible facts placed out without inventing practice attempts until real attempts take over.
- The Tutorial is an in-app stepper that highlights real controls and explains problem types, typing rules, Speed/Drops/Text Size controls, mastery grids, Spacebar Breather, boss mode, Test Me, and local profiles/session logs.
- Welcome-menu and Tutorial copy lives in `src/text/english.js` so it can be edited in one place and later mirrored into other locale files.
- Immediate-clear operations: add, subtract, multiply, divide, factors of 10, Rounding (level-gated nearest tens/hundreds/thousands and tenths/hundredths/thousandths, with mastery buckets for rounding cases like down/up/half/zero/carry scoped by place and size relationship), Shapes (level-gated: 2D square/rectangle/triangle/circle perimeter & area, then 3D cube/rectangular-prism/cylinder/sphere surface area & volume; round shapes answer as the π coefficient), and Powers & Roots (level-gated: squares & square roots, powers & roots of 10, powers of 2 & 3, cubes & cube roots, and negative powers of 10; all answers are whole numbers or clean decimals).
- Enter-required operations: SI metric conversions such as `*1000` or `/100`, and full prime factorization answers such as `2^2*3`.
- Rapid impossible submissions briefly overload the cannon and ignore input for a short cooldown; partial answers and editing do not count, and the overload is not recorded as mastery accuracy. The overload is **answer-space-aware**: on levels with only a few possible answers (e.g. L1 subtraction = {0,1,2}) it heats up faster, so the cannon can't be brute-forced by cycling the small answer set. (It curbs *sustained* guessing; an isolated lucky clear can still slip through.)
- Prime factor drops can be targeted with Tab and simplified one factor at a time; when factoring is the only operation in play the most urgent drop is auto-targeted, so you can either step through factors or type the full `2^2*3` and press Enter.
- Each operation can be toggled on/off during gameplay; if none are enabled, no new drops spawn. The operation chits show current course progress as a subtle fill and their hover tips include the current level. Operations are grouped into compatible sets (arithmetic `+ - × ÷` and `×10` together; Rounding; Shapes; SI; Factoring); enabling an operation from a different set disables the incompatible ones.
- Each operation has an independent difficulty level from 1-10, starting at level 1 for new profiles.
- Current levels are stored in the local profile; increasing to the next level requires beating that operation's boss mode at the current level.
- Boss mode unlocks from the per-operation Mastered control once 100% of the current-level problem universe is mastered. A problem counts as mastered only after at least 3 attempts with at least 90% current accuracy, where current accuracy heavily weights recent performance while retaining a small lifetime correction so older mistakes fade without trapping a player forever. Once an operation reaches 80% but still has unfinished current-level facts, ordinary practice strongly favors the remaining blockers so the final stretch feels intentional rather than random. Once mastered, the Mastered control lightly pulses and a modal choice temporarily interrupts play with Keep Practicing, Boss, and Next Level options. Choosing Next Level records a mastery advance separately from boss clears, so boss remains optional. On reload, each operation resumes at the level after its highest boss clear or mastery advance so a temporarily lowered selector does not strand progress.
- Full boss mode runs three assessment stages for the selected operation and level: Wave 1 is a shield-endurance drop-time ramp with a fixed simultaneous-bomb cap, Wave 2 is a load-ladder that presents a round of N problems and only steps up to N+1 after the whole round is cleared, and the final mothership is a Worksheet covering the entire current-level problem universe split across sequential Shields, Guns, Wings, and Core parts. The mothership reveals a small capped batch of problems at a time (at most 6 visible, never two with the same answer) so a typed answer can never clear the wrong node; solving all nodes on a part knocks that part off as fading damaged debris, and parts left empty by a small universe auto-collapse. Worksheet missiles drop from visible remaining ship nodes; solving a missile also clears that source node, so missiles add pressure without adding extra required facts.
- Wave 1 and standalone Blitz are open-ended shield-endurance runs that save survival time plus the fastest drop time reached; the old 85% pressure point is now just the baseline before the ramp keeps increasing more gradually. Wave 2 saves the highest simultaneous load fully cleared, while still tracking solved counts in attempt history.
- Clearing the final mothership advances that operation to the next content level and unlocks replay challenges for the cleared level: Blitz survival time, Wave max cleared load, and Worksheet time. Mastery-only advancement also unlocks revisiting that level for standalone Blitz, Wave, or Worksheet attempts without forcing all three stages.
- Boss, Blitz, and Wave problems are assessment/challenge events: they do not update ordinary per-problem mastery stats or the session Cleared counter.
- Blitz, Wave, and Worksheet buttons appear when the selected level is already unlocked behind the current working level, or when the current level is fully mastered and the player chooses to keep practicing instead of advancing. Blitz is a shield-endurance survival run; Wave is a simultaneous-load run; Worksheet records time without advancing content level. Higher-level challenge results can supersede lower-level displayed bests when they are better.
- Global practice controls include Speed from 0-100%, Drops from 0-10 simultaneous falling problems, and a persisted Text Size setting for larger falling-drop and worksheet-node problem text.
- Pause stops motion and spawning without covering the playfield, so settings, grids, and the Log remain accessible.
- Finish clears the active board, disables the selected problem types, stamps the current session, and opens that sitting's Report with Share visible. It does not hard-close the session; if the player turns an operation back on, the new work stays in the same report until the normal idle/session boundary.
- Space starts a practice-only Breather: visible drops stop moving, no new drops spawn, and normal play resumes automatically once the visible board is cleared. The input hint calls this out during play.
- The header shows a session-only Cleared counter for correct drops cleared during the current run.
- The Log link opens a local session history. A sitting stays in one session across brief reloads or returns: startup resumes the active profile's newest session when its `lastSeenAt` is within 30 minutes, while a longer gap or player switch starts a fresh report. Session headline duration is idle-capped active time, so a tab left open for hours does not inflate a report; legacy sessions fall back to summed per-operation engaged time. Sessions record practice stats, boss/challenge solved counts, challenge starts/completions, challenge activity by type (Blitz/Wave/Worksheet), and a Report view with per-operation engaged duration, correct/missed counts, challenge breakdowns, and level-by-level mastery start/end changes. Boss pressure is still kept out of ordinary mastery accuracy.
- The Login link opens the local player manager where players can create or switch named profiles stored in localStorage, clear the current player's saved practice stats, or back up / restore the active player's full profile with a compressed checksummed code or text file.
- A session Report has a "Share with a parent" button that opens the native share sheet (or copies a link) carrying a compact read-only report snapshot of just that session (no server, report values encoded and compressed in the link `#report=`). Finish opens this same Report directly for the current sitting. Opening a shared link lands the parent straight on the same Report popup in a read-only shared view (banner, no editing, an Exit button), reusing the normal report rendering against the decoded report model.
- A local profile tracks per-operation readiness, per-problem outcomes, saved Speed/Drops/Text Size settings, derived pressure-tier practice metadata, boss clears, Blitz/Wave/Boss challenge attempts and bests, and boss-readiness recommendations in localStorage. Active-player backup/restore exports that same profile shape so it can later migrate through the normal profile loader.
- Existing single-profile localStorage data is migrated into the local profile selector as `david` when it still has the old default player identity.
- Mastery readiness is universe-aware: it is the percentage of current-level problems that meet the 3-attempt/90%-current-accuracy mastery rule or have active placed-out credit from Test Me, with Boss/Next Level choices unlocked at 100% and focused finish practice starting at 80%.
- Current-level mastery is visible on per-operation level controls; detailed per-problem progress is shown through each operation's Grid and session Reports.
- Gated level and boss/challenge controls explain why they are locked on both desktop and touch, instead of relying on hover-only titles or silent no-ops.
- Falling practice drops are weighted toward unmastered, low-accuracy, and under-attempted problems; above 80% current-level mastery, most practice drops for that operation come from the remaining unmastered current-level facts.
- Problem accuracy is shown through per-operation stats popups. Hovering/focusing grid cells shows detailed attempts, accuracy, mastery status, and Test Me placed-out credit. Operation level cards include a Grid hint that opens the per-problem accuracy grid/list.
- Falling drops are shaded with the same accuracy/evidence palette as the stats popup: untested problems are black, hue runs from red through yellow to green by accuracy, brightness/opacity increases with repeated attempts, and placed-out facts are green placement credit until enough later real attempts override that estimate.
- During boss/challenge play the header readout (normally the session Cleared count) shows live stage progress instead: Blitz survival time and current drop time, Wave best cleared load/current load, and the Worksheet's cleared/total count plus elapsed time. Between failed shield waves, a screen-wide bottom-to-top laser sweep represents the player's super weapon clearing the rest of the wave before the next stage begins. Operation toggles, level controls, and challenge controls are locked while a boss/challenge is active. A full boss clear opens a victory summary (congratulations, the three stage results, Next Level, Grid, and Recap buttons), with the accuracy grid reachable from it. The Recap popup shows/copies the best Blitz, Wave, and Worksheet stats for that operation level and uses the browser share sheet when available. A parallax starfield and a mothership that looms closer each wave reinforce the sense of approaching the boss.
- Touch devices use an on-screen keypad and compact controls. Operation chits wrap into a visible grid instead of a horizontal scroller, and short landscape layouts put the keypad beside the playfield to preserve vertical room.
- Physical numpad entry is normalized so bumping NumLock does not break answer entry; numpad digits still enter numbers when the browser reports them as navigation keys.
- Donate links point to the donation page from the header/menu and from each session report; Feedback opens a FormSubmit-backed feedback form.

## Non-Goals
- No lives, timers, ELO rating, backend sync, or build pipeline for production.
- Login is currently local profile selection only; it is not authentication.
- Tests and Playwright are dev-only tooling; the deployed game remains static.
